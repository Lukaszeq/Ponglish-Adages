About this website

Eight hundred years of development.

Data gathered from 60 million libraries from three different planets.

Two time travels (to Geoffrey Chaucer and to prince Popiel).

Countless glasses of Apology of Socrates. 3 cl ouzo, 1 cl club soda, 1 dash poison hemlock extract. Shake all ingredients in a shaker filled with ice. Strain into a cocktail glass. Drink in a bathtub as courtesy to the coroner and the cleaning service.

"Life doesn't make any sense, and we all pretend it does", said Eric Idle. Aficionados of measurable truths may struggle with lowering their shields, taking an unfiltered look at the world, and trying not to explain every peculiarity with rationalism. Counterweighting nonsense with sanity is futile. Thus, it is not unheard of a humanist to ultimately become a prophet of doom, praying for an asteroid on a collision course with the Earth every time one finishes reading a morning newspaper. What makes me keep a degree of equanimity is doubling down on absurd, fending off preposterous happenings with tomfoolery. And this is why I asked Jesus to turn a two-day-old egg-salad sandwich into the website (hence the color scheme).
